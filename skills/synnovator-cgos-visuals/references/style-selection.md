# Style Selection

## Decision Inputs

Judge style from the project before choosing imagery:

- industry and competition track;
- product mechanism and interface/product form;
- maturity and evidence level;
- audience: judges, investors, users, partners, or media;
- communication goal: explain, impress, recruit, announce, or summarize;
- existing logo, colors, screenshots, product object, and character/IP;
- the strongest honest visual metaphor for how the product works.

Do not select style from the word “AI” alone.

## Contemporary Style Catalog

| Style | Best fit | Visual signals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Contemporary tech editorial | Software, agents, developer tools | Bold typography, interface fragments, abstract mechanism diagrams, disciplined grid |
| Editorial industrial systems | Manufacturing, supply chain, robotics, energy | Process slices, equipment/data relationship, operational maps, high-contrast technical editorial composition |
| Contemporary Swiss technology | Infrastructure, B2B, professional services | Strong grid, typographic scale, precise modules, restrained color |
| Bold typographic editorial poster | A project with a strong name or proposition | Large custom title, controlled negative space, one clear metaphor |
| Neo-brutalist technology | Youthful experimental tools and community products | Hard borders, strong type, asymmetric modules, raw but intentional composition |
| Editorial data narrative | Analytics, finance, evaluation, research | Data relationships, diagrammatic hierarchy, credible editorial tone |
| Minimal product campaign | Consumer, retail, hardware, lifestyle | Clear product hero, clean scene, small number of branded graphic devices |
| Contemporary editorial illustration | Culture, education, social impact, services | Human-centered narrative, custom metaphor, modern publishing composition |
| Youth competition campaign | Recruitment, demo day, awards, project showcases | Fast hierarchy, expressive title, project identity, event energy without generic esports effects |

Choose one primary style. Add at most one supporting influence when it solves a specific communication need.

## Forbidden Styles

Reject these directions even when they are described as “futuristic” or “premium”:

- cheap blue-purple gradients;
- generic glassmorphism;
- metallic sci-fi surfaces and excessive glow;
- stale cyberpunk/neon city imagery;
- government notice or red-header document layout;
- corporate presentation/dashboard poster layout;
- ecommerce detail-page composition;
- meaningless particles, network globes, circuit backgrounds, and light trails;
- generic AI robots, brains, chips, hands touching screens, or humanoid assistants;
- colored slabs, duplicate plates, or loose offset blocks underneath cards and panels;
- stock-template collage or “replace title and colors” reuse;
- overdecorated title shadows instead of real typographic transformation.

Use strokes, inner rails, attached contour bands, foreground labels, icon fills, and internal contrast to create depth. Do not put colored blocks below surfaces.

## Style Rationale Format

Write two sentences:

1. Name the project mechanism, audience, and selected direction.
2. Explain the concrete visual devices that make this project recognizable and distinguish it from generic AI imagery.

Bad: “科技感适合这个 AI 项目。”

Good: “FlowPilot 面向制造业评委和投资人，核心机制是排程与异常预警，因此采用工业系统编辑视觉。画面用生产流程切片、告警节点与产品界面局部建立识别度，避免机器人、芯片和蓝紫发光网络。”
